EXIT STAGES — USE THESE ACCURATELY
Closed Lost
Deal is definitively dead. Client has confirmed they're not proceeding. Log the reason in the notes field — this data feeds into pipeline reviews.

Processes & Docs

Key processes, workflows, and documentation standards from the #bag-chasers canvases. Click each card to expand for detail.

PRE-SALES WORKFLOW
💛 Team Values
Core principles that guide how we operate
▼
  • Act as a team and support each other
  • Slack is the single source of truth
  • Clean handovers across time zones; ensure offline updates are clear
  • Self-policing culture — knowledge sharing is key
  • Strong foundations and values should minimise escalations
📋 Pre-Sale Process — Under £150K
Standard process for deals below the £150K threshold
▼
  • Solutions team to forefront the response
  • Join Slack group chat, attend daily stand-up, pull insights, work through brief
  • Links back to final Sales proposal — oversight before it goes out the door; numbers must make sense
📋 Pre-Sale Process — Over £150K
Enhanced process for high-value deals requiring creative & strategy
▼
  • Creative & Strategy team involved; more custom production
  • Need integrated marketing support for channels and content mix
  • Strategy + Creative to lead with clear ownership lines
  • 24-hour check-in needs to happen earlier in process
  • Earlier internal kick-off before deck build
  • Greater presence during deck build to avoid misalignment
🗓️ Daily Stand-Up
Stand-up format and attendance rules
▼
  • Focus on amplification
  • For deals under £150K — join if custom elements are involved
📊 Media Planner Role
When and how the media planner is involved
▼
  • Needs to join earlier (creative stage)
  • Should understand what is going out from the beginning
  • Role includes packaging, paid placement, execution alignment, 24-hour check consistency
  • Minimum 3-month publisher contracts
  • Pre-sale focused but 360 experience (pre + post-sale)
💡 Proactive Work
Ideas Bank, Springboard, and keeping materials fresh
▼
  • Put proactive ideas in front of prospective clients
  • Create internal Ideas Bank (add to Sales group)
  • "Springboard" overview for efficiency
  • Ensure materials are up to date
💰 Cost of Sale
Budget minimums, evaluation criteria, and strategic value assessment
▼
  • Increased demand — enforce minimum budget levels
  • Sales targets linked to annual OKRs
  • Evaluate: turnaround time, soft costs, strategic value, new vertical entry
  • Review CRM history before accepting
  • Encourage Sales to push back when budget doesn't meet objectives
  • Example: Apple £150K — declined as it wouldn't meet objectives
  • Long-term strategic partnerships > short-term revenue
⏱️ SLAs
Lead times, turnaround expectations, and pushback guidelines
▼
  • Define lead times
  • Not everything can be same-day turnaround
  • Empower teams to push back appropriately
  • Exceptions only for major revenue-driving clients
  • High demand is positive — but not at cost of wellbeing
🚫 Ad Policy
Prohibited brands, restricted categories, and frequency guidelines
▼
  • Prohibited brands list
  • Restricted categories
  • Ad frequency guidelines by platform + cadence
  • Mandatory agreements: ensure all live content is paid for
🧹 Channel & Documentation Hygiene
Naming conventions, HubSpot sync, and metadata standards
▼
  • Standardised naming conventions (dash vs underscore consistency)
  • HubSpot synced
  • Project code must remain consistent; same ID across teams
  • Campaign name should act as metadata

Lost Deals & Learnings

Understanding why deals are lost is just as valuable as winning them. Always log the reason in HubSpot.

📋 HubSpot Closed Lost Reasons
Dropdown options available when marking a deal as Closed Lost
▼
Budget pulled Price (too expensive) Went with another publisher Media planning changed Creative idea didn't land Strategy misalignment Demographics didn't fit Not brand safe Deadline missed Tight timings No response from client We opted out Too many political posts on the grid Reason not given
🧠 Key Learnings from Lost Deals
Patterns and takeaways from pipeline reviews
▼
  • Always log the Closed Lost Reason in HubSpot — this feeds pipeline reviews
  • Budget pulled is the #1 reason for lost deals — often out of our control
  • "Price too expensive" — check if we're pitching the right product tier for their budget
  • "Went with another publisher" — debrief with Rachel; what did they offer that we didn't?
  • "No response" — use the "No Response From Stakeholder" exit stage, don't leave deals stuck
  • Review CRM history before accepting low-budget briefs (Cost of Sale evaluation)
📝 Process for Logging Lost Deals
Step-by-step process when a deal is lost
▼
  1. Move deal to Closed Lost in HubSpot immediately — don't leave it sitting
  2. Select all applicable Closed Lost Reasons (multiple can be selected)
  3. Add notes explaining what happened
  4. Automation will set Closed Won Reason to "N/A - Closed Lost" automatically
  5. Share learnings in weekly pipeline review
